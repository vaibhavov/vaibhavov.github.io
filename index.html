About

I grew up fascinated by maps — the way they collapse the world into something you can hold and reason about.

That fascination grew into a career at the edge of geospatial science and AI. My undergrad in Civil Engineering at IIT Roorkee gave me physical intuition; my master's at IIT Bombay gave me the tools to push further — under Prof. Surya S. Durbha at CSRE, one of India's leading remote sensing labs.

I like working on problems where the data is messy and the stakes are real. Like estimating crop yield from radar backscatter in a district where farmers make decisions on gut feel. If I can replace some of that uncertainty with signal, that's worth building for.

Outside research: I cook with the same experimental energy I bring to model architectures. I sing. I build things with my hands. And I compete in programming contests when I need a reminder that elegance matters.

Projects

Things I've built

01
LangGraph GEE RAG Ollama

GeoEye — an AI that thinks like an agronomist

A multi-agent framework that pulls live Sentinel satellite data, runs it through a crop-science RAG pipeline built on ChromaDB and Qwen3 embeddings, and hands farmers decision-ready intelligence. The whole thing runs locally on an RTX A5000 — no cloud dependency. Built on LangGraph with a shared AgentState schema across Master, Data, Monitoring, Yield, and Decision sub-agents.

02
Sentinel-1 PyTorch SSL 3D-CNN

Soybean yield from radar shadows

SAR backscatter doesn't look like anything to a human eye — but a 3D-CNN trained on fortnightly Sentinel-1 composites over Latur district can predict field-level yield. The hard part: only 568 labeled fields. The solution: a self-supervised pretraining stage using masked temporal autoencoding, giving the model structure before it ever sees a yield label.

View project → Code →
03
YOLOv8 Kalman Filter SAHI

Tracking a cricket ball at 140 km/h

A physics-aware Kalman tracker built on YOLOv8x + SAHI detection. The interesting problem wasn't detection — it was false-positive suppression. A ball in flight looks a lot like a bird, a shadow, a player's arm. The solution was a state machine (IDLE / TRACKING / LOST) that gates detections through Euclidean physics constraints before committing to a track.

04
Prithvi 300M Fine-tuning TCS Research

Mowing detection from orbit — TCS Research internship

Fine-tuned the Prithvi 300M foundation model on German grassland satellite data to detect mowing events from spectral time series. Reached 87% accuracy on the held-out test set. This project got me thinking seriously about what domain-specific pretraining actually buys you.

Interests

What pulls me in

I think about geospatial AI, agentic systems, geoinformatics and the gap between foundation models and real-world Earth observation. I am always amazed by the diverse applicability of this domain.

Geospatial AI

Remote sensing data is noisy, incomplete, and full of latent structure. Designing models that respect that structure — rather than flattening it into a feature vector — is where I spend most of my thinking time.

Agentic systems

LLMs that can plan, retrieve, and reason over satellite data and domain knowledge are still mostly unsolved. GeoEye is my attempt at bridging that gap in a domain where decisions actually matter.

Competitive programming

Contests are an honest mirror. They show you quickly whether your understanding is deep or just fluent. I compete to stay sharp and because there is a certain joy in an elegant proof.

Making things

Code, food, music, furniture. The medium changes; the obsession with craft doesn't. Building tangible things keeps research honest — you either made it work or you didn't.
